Overview

Glaucoma is caused by increased pressure inside the eye that damages the optic nerve. The result is often the inverse of the effect of macular degeneration: loss of peripheral vision and blurry vision in the center. Text will be difficult to read. Some people have compared glaucoma to looking at everything through a straw.

Symptoms

Symptoms of glaucoma may include blurred vision, eye pain, headache, nausea, and vision loss.

Diagnosis

Glaucoma can be diagnosed through comprehensive eye exams, including tonometry, visual field tests, and optic nerve imaging.

Treatment

Treatment for glaucoma may include prescription eye drops, oral medications, laser therapy, or surgery.

Self-Care

Self-care measures for glaucoma include regular eye exams, taking prescribed medications as directed, protecting the eyes from injury, and maintaining a healthy lifestyle.
